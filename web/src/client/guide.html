Map buttons

The buttons run down the right edge of the map, top to bottom. Zoom sits top-left.

  • SearchOpens a search box at the top to find places by name.
  • LayersOpens the menu to pick a base map and toggle overlays.
  • RecordStarts recording a track. Turns into a stop button while recording.
  • My locationCenters the map on your current position.
  • RulerTurns the tap-to-measure ruler on and off. Highlights when on.
  • SettingsOpens Settings.
  • + / − ZoomTop-left. Zoom in and out one step at a time.

Finding your way

  • Move the mapDrag to pan, pinch to zoom, twist with two fingers to rotate.
  • Go to meTap My location to jump to where you are.
  • SearchTap Search, type a place, and tap a result to fly there.
  • Recent searchesOpen search with the box empty to see your recent places.

Layers

Tap the Layers button. Pick one base map (marked with a check); toggle any number of overlays.

Base maps — pick one

  • Kartverket – official Norwegian topographic map
  • Kartverket toporaster – raster version of the topo map
  • Kartverket sjøkart – nautical charts
  • MapAnt – orienteering maps from laser scans
  • OpenStreetMap – worldwide community map
  • OpenTopoMap – global topo map with hiking trails
  • Satellite (Sentinel-2) – cloud-free satellite imagery

Overlays — toggle any

  • Waymarked TrailsMarked hiking routes.
  • Avalanche ZonesSteep-terrain runout areas from NVE.
  • HillshadeShaded relief for a sense of terrain.
  • Saved PointsShows or hides your saved places.
  • Coordinate GridDraws a reference grid over the map.
  • CrosshairCenter reticle plus a live position readout (see below).

Crosshair & coordinates

Turn on Crosshair in the layers menu to read off any point.

  • ReticleA crosshair marks the exact center of the map.
  • Readout cardBottom-left: coordinates, elevation, slope, and distance from you.
  • Switch formatTap the coordinates to cycle Lat/Lng → DMS → UTM → MGRS.
  • Set a defaultSettings → Coordinate Format sets which one is used everywhere.

Measuring distance

Two-finger measure — the quick way

  • MeasureTap the map with two fingers at once. A dashed line and the straight-line distance between them appear.
  • ClearIt fades away on its own after a few seconds.

Ruler — for multi-point routes

  • Turn onTap the Ruler button. A card appears reading “Tap to measure”.
  • Add pointsTap along the map. The card shows running total distance and point count.
  • Undo / clearUse the buttons on the card to remove the last point or clear all.
  • Keep itWith 2+ points, tap Save as Track to store the line as a track.
  • Turn offTap the Ruler button again.

Recording a track

  • StartTap Record. On first use, allow background location so it keeps logging with the screen off.
  • While recordingA red card shows live distance; a notification keeps it running in the background.
  • StopTap the button again (now a stop square).
  • SaveName the track and tap Save — or Discard to throw it away.

Saved tracks

Open Settings → Saved Tracks for everything you have recorded or imported.

  • Show on MapOpens the track, fitted to the screen, with a banner naming it.
  • RenameEdit the track name.
  • ExportSave as a standard GPX file.
  • ShareSend it through the system share sheet.
  • DeleteRemove the track.

Importing tracks

  • From a fileOpen a GPX or FIT file from your device or another app.
  • From a linkIn Settings → Saved Tracks, paste a link from Ut.no, Garmin Connect, Komoot, or Strava — or any URL ending in .gpx or .fit.
  • ConfirmYou preview the track before it is added.

Saved points

  • Save a pointPress and hold a spot on the map, name it, and tap Save.
  • EditTap a marker to change its name, description, or color.
  • DeleteUse Delete in that same dialog.
  • Manage allSettings → Saved Points lists them; tap any to jump to it.
  • Show / hideToggle Saved Points in the layers menu.

Live location sharing

Let people follow you in real time in any web browser — no account needed. Open Settings → Online Tracking.

Share your location

  • Turn onEnable online tracking. You get a short client ID.
  • Start sharingSwitch on sharing and pick a duration (15 min up to 8 hours).
  • Send the linkUse Share Tracking Link, or View on Web to open it yourself.
  • Who's watchingA chip on the map shows the viewer count and time left.
  • New IDRegenerate the client ID to retire the old link.

Follow someone

  • FollowEnter their 6-character client ID and tap Follow.
  • On the mapA banner shows their status; their track draws live. Tap it to re-center.
  • StopTap the X on the banner, or Stop Following.
On the web: open where.synth.no to watch live tracks in any browser, filter by client ID, and view recent tracks from the last day.

Offline maps

Download map areas so they work with no signal. Open Settings → Offline Maps and pick a layer.

  • DownloadTap the hexagons over the area you want, then tap Download. A progress bar appears at the top.
  • Hex colorsGray = not downloaded, green = done, and partial areas can be continued.
  • Elevation dataOptionally include altitude and slope so the crosshair readout works offline.
  • ManageSee size per layer, delete a layer, or clear the tile cache.
  • Offline ModeThe Offline Mode switch stops all network use, relying only on downloads.

Settings

  • ThemeLight, dark, or follow the system.
  • LanguageEnglish, Norwegian, or your device default.
  • Coordinate FormatYour default of Lat/Lng, DMS, UTM, or MGRS.
  • Offline ModeUse only downloaded maps and data.
  • Crash reportingOptionally send anonymous reports to help fix bugs.
  • AttributionsThe map sources and open-source libraries behind Where?
Private by design: no accounts, no ads, no analytics profiles. Shared location data is anonymous and short-lived.
